Ola Analysis

Ola Analysis involves a detailed examination of Ola Cabs, a leading ride-hailing service, to uncover insights into its operational efficiency, market performance, customer behavior, and strategic positioning.


By performing this analysis, stakeholders can identify areas for improvement, forecast future performance, and make data-driven decisions to enhance Ola's competitive edge and profitability.

SQl Queries For Analysis

Retrieve all successful bookings:

To determine the number of successful bookings, we can use this straightforward query for analysis. 

            
CREATE VIEW Successful_Booking AS
SELECT *
FROM bookings
WHERE booking_status = "Success";
List the top 5 customers who successfully booked the highest number of rides:

The purpose of identifying the top 5 customers who successfully booked the highest number of rides is to recognize your most frequent and loyal users. This insight can help tailor marketing strategies, offer personalized rewards, and improve customer retention by focusing on high-value customers.

  SELECT Customer_ID, COUNT(booking_ID) AS 
  Highest_Number_Of_Rides
  FROM bookings
  WHERE booking_status = "Success"
  GROUP BY Customer_ID
  ORDER BY Highest_Number_Of_Rides DESC
  LIMIT 5;
Retrieve all rides where payment was made using UPI

The purpose of this query is to analyze bookings made using UPI as the payment method. By filtering data in this way, businesses can gain insights into the usage patterns and popularity of UPI among customers.

  SELECT *
  FROM bookings
  WHERE Payment_Method = "UPI";

Insights For Vehicle's Performance

The below visualization displays booking data for various vehicle types on the Ola app, including total booking value, successful booking value, average distance traveled, and total distance traveled. It shows that Prime SUV has the highest booking value, followed by Prime Plus and E-Bike. Most vehicles have an average distance traveled of approximately 25 km, with Prime Sedan having the highest total distance traveled.

Insights For Cancelled Rides

The graph illustrates cancellation rates for rides on the OLA ride-hailing platform, broken down by cancellation reasons. The most frequent reason, making up 29.36% of cancellations, is "More than permitted people in there." The second most common reason, "Driver asked to cancel," accounts for 25.43% of cancellations. This data indicates that the company should focus on enhancing the driver onboarding process to ensure drivers are well-informed about the terms of service and properly trained. Additionally, improving communication with customers regarding ride restrictions, such as the number of passengers allowed, may help reduce cancellations.
